sort1 uses: Bubble

How do you know?: sorted50000 ran in only 1.4sec, however Reversed and Random both took over 6sec. With sorted text the omega would be equal to N.

sort2 uses: Merge

How do you know?: Across all 50000 datasets this was the fastest algo by a factor of at least 100%. Across the random5000 dataset however that gap closed considerably.

sort3 uses: Selection

How do you know?: This algo performed consistantly across all 50000 datasets, it performed better than sort1 when the data was random or reversed, but considerably worse when that data was sorted.
